A GPT-2 Based Controllable Text Colloquialization Framework for Converting Written Text to Spoken Style with Streaming Input in Mandarin Speech Synthesis

Anonymous submission to ASRU 2025

Abstract: In this paper, we propose a GPT-based text colloquialization framework to enhance the spontaneity of input text, in order to improve the naturalness of synthesized speech. The structure of GPT with only Transformer decoder is utilized to learn the conversion from reference text to colloquial text. Additionally, the Word Error Rate (WER) metric is applied to quantify the degree of disfluency insertion. During training, a corresponding special token is incorporated to allow manual degree control of colloquialization. To further decrease the first-package latency, a streaming input training strategy is introduced to resolve the mismatches between training and inference in streaming input mode. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed approach effectively improves the naturalness of synthesized speech and provides strong control over discourse marker insertion, particularly for sentences with lengths between medium and long.
